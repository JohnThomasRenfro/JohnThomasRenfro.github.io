Russian Grammar Cases & Agreements

Nominative Case

Identifies the subject; answers who or what is acting.

The cat sleeps. (Кот спит.)

genitive Case

Shows possession or origin; answers from where or whose.

Anna’s book. (Книга Анны.)

Accusative Case

Marks the direct object or direction; answers where to or what is acted on.

I see the dog. (Я вижу собаку.)

Prepositional Case

Used with locations or topics; answers where at, in, or about whom/what.

We talk about school. (Мы говорим о школе.)

Dative Case

Indicates the indirect object; answers to whom or for what.

I give a gift to a friend. (Я даю подарок другу.)

Instrumental Case

Expresses means or accompaniment; answers by whom or with what.

I write with a pencil. (Я пишу карандашом.)

Agreements in Russian

Adjective-Noun Agreement

Adjectives must match their nouns in gender, number, and case.

Red car → Красная машина (Both are feminine and singular).

Verb Agreement

Verbs do not change based on noun case—only by person and number.

Prepositional Agreement

Prepositions require specific cases, affecting meaning:

  • в школе → in school (uses Prepositional)
  • с другом → with a friend (uses Instrumental)
